# Research Framing Skill

Use this skill before writing the survey.

## Protocol

1. Define the topic boundary in evidence-grounded terms.
2. State 3-5 research questions that can be answered from retrieved papers.
3. Separate what is in scope from what is out of scope.
4. Identify the organizing thesis of the review.
5. Prefer comparison axes that are visible in evidence: method family, modeled object, evaluation target, limitation, and future direction.

## Constraints

- Do not introduce papers that are not in the evidence KB.
- Do not treat a broad topic phrase as a factual claim.
- If retrieved evidence is thin, narrow the scope instead of inventing coverage.
